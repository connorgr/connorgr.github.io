What do I do?

I study visualization and human-computer interaction as a computer science PhD student at Brown University. Before coming to Brown I graduated from Tufts University with a B.S. in computer science and a minor in religious studies.

Outside of academia I love designing things and am fascinated by music. I recently traveled to Ghana to continue my education about Ewe dance and music.

Work Experience

Brown University
Graduate student, 2012 – Current
I work on human-computer interaction and visualization research. Each of my current projects focuses on one of three areas: perception and visualization, cancer genomics visualization, and quantified self systems research. My advisor is Professor David Laidlaw, and I have ongoing collaborations with Professors Karen Schloss, Ben Raphael, and Jeff Huang.

Google
Software Engineer Intern, Summer 2012
During my time at Google I worked on Ripples, an interactive content discovery visualization for Google+. As part of my internship I designed the scope of a new feature, gained experience doing back-end, middleware, and front-end programming, and constructed the visual design of the feature.

Charles River Analytics
Software Engineer/Scientist Intern, Summer 2011
At Charles River Analytics I worked on creating a platform for controlling multiple, heterogeneous unmanned vehicles. I built the prototype from the ground up, including much of the front-end feature specification. I also worked on a usability study to learn about effective techniques for emergency response management.

Tufts University
Undergraduate Research Assistant, 2010 – 2012
Teaching Assistant, 2010 – 2012
While at Tufts I conducted my own visualization research on various projects under the advisement of Professors Remco Chang, Lenore Cowen, Sam Guyer, and Dr. Sara Su. I’ve performed visualization research in proteomics and protein bioinformatics, software analysis and debugging, and computer graphics.
In addition to research, I also helped teach courses in the computer science department. I was the lead teaching assitant for computer graphics, as well as a lab instructor and teaching assistant for data structures, the second course in Tufts' computer science major.
