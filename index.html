I am a PhD candidate and NSF Graduate Research Fellow at Brown's computer science department, where I focus on visualization and human-computer interaction, advised by David Laidlaw.

I am specializing in genomics visualization and graphical perception.

I approach visualization research through the lens of a toolsmith. I find myself at home when collaborating, where I help advance other areas of research while identifying generalizable visualization and HCI contributions.

To this end, I collaborate with Ben Raphael (cancer genomics) and Karen Schloss (perception). I have also collaborated with Jeff Huang (HCI) on complimentary crowdsourcing research.


I recently obtained my transitional ScM in computer science from Brown. Before Brown, I was an undergraduate at Tufts University where I worked with Remco Chang and Sara Su. My undergraduate research ranged from protein structure depiction to exploring data structures in Java heaps. I also minored in (secular) religious studies.
