The relation between grouping, size, and set size
  • Connor C. Gramazio
  • Karen B. Schloss
  • David H. Laidlaw

In this paper we make the following contributions: (1) we describe how the grouping, quantity, and size of visual marks affects search time based on the results from two experiments; (2) we report how search performance relates to self-reported difficulty in finding the target for different display types; and (3) we present design guidelines based on our findings to facilitate the design of effective visualizations.
